Grade 4 – Grade 8

Basic Level Education

Our Basic Level program incorporates the Playway Method and Learning by Doing approach — promoting cognitive and social development through interactive, hands-on activities.

Students in Grade 4–8 are encouraged to think independently, collaborate with peers, and explore subjects through project-based, real-world tasks that build academic confidence and character.

Teaching Methodology

How We Teach

Playway Method

The Playway Method integrates purposeful play into structured learning, helping students in the Basic Level develop their cognitive, motor, and social skills simultaneously. Through games, creative activities, and guided exploration, children gain academic knowledge in a joyful, pressure-free environment that builds their confidence and intrinsic motivation to learn.

Learning by Doing

Our "Learning by Doing" philosophy encourages students to gain knowledge and skills through direct experience and reflection. Rather than passive instruction, students conduct experiments, build models, collaborate on projects, and engage in field-based learning. This experiential approach develops problem-solving ability, teamwork, and real-world readiness that lasts a lifetime.

STEAM Integration

Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics are woven throughout the curriculum, giving students a well-rounded, interdisciplinary education. Our annual STEAM Exhibition showcases student creativity, curiosity, and innovation — students present self-directed projects that address real-world challenges and demonstrate their analytical and creative abilities.

ECA & Co-curricular

Extra-Curricular Activities (ECA) are central to the Basic Level experience. Students participate in sports, arts, music, dance, debate, and community service. These activities develop social skills, resilience, and a well-rounded character. Our ECA programs are structured to complement academic learning and ensure every student discovers their talents and passions.
